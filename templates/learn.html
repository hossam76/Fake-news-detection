Understand the impact of fake news and how to identify it

What is Fake News?

Fake news refers to false or misleading information presented as factual news. It is often created to:

  • Influence political views
  • Generate website traffic
  • Spread misinformation
  • Manipulate public perception

How to Spot Fake News

  • Check the source of the news
  • Read beyond the headlines
  • Look for unusual URLs
  • Verify the date and facts
  • Cross-check with other reputable sources

How Our System Works

Our Fake News Detection System uses advanced machine learning and natural language processing techniques to analyze news content. Here's how it works:

  1. Input: Users submit text or a URL for analysis.
  2. Preprocessing: The text is cleaned and normalized.
  3. Analysis: Machine learning models process the content to classify it as real or fake.
  4. Results: The system provides a classification with a confidence score.
